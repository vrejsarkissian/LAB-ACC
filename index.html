Distribution Rule
Contributions are flat: a waiter's band rate is charged on their whole collected total. To prevent the cliff at the threshold, a floor protection applies — if charging a contributor in full would drop their take-home below the most any lower-collecting waiter takes home, their tip-out is trimmed just enough to keep them level (shown as capped). After the kitchen and manager tipouts, the remaining contribution pool is used to level up the waiters who collected less than $130 (the first bracket ceiling): the lowest earners are brought up first toward a common floor, so the further below the line someone fell, the more they receive. No one below the line is ever raised past someone who collected more.
